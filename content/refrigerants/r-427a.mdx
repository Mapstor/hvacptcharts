narrative:
  whatItIs: |
    R-427A is Arkema's four-component R-22 retrofit blend, developed in the 2000s alongside the other R-22 retrofit families (Chemours' ISCEON line, Honeywell's Genetron MO99). The composition includes 15% R-32 — the higher-volatility HFC component that contributes capacity — alongside the R-125, R-143a, and R-134a components that provide the operating envelope and non-flammability.

    Unlike the hydrocarbon-aided R-22 retrofits (R-417A, R-422A, R-422D, R-438A), R-427A achieves mineral-oil compatibility through blend composition rather than a hydrocarbon additive. Arkema markets this as oil-flexibility — R-427A works with MO, AB, or POE — so the retrofit doesn't require an oil change but POE can be added if preferred.

  whereItsUsed:
    - R-22 retrofit (residential AC, heat pumps, light commercial)
    - Medium-temperature commercial refrigeration (R-22 retrofit)
    - Some compressor manufacturers' R-22 service literature names R-427A as a preferred retrofit

  phaseDownStatus: |
    R-427A's GWP of 2138 is above the EPA AIM Act 700 threshold for new equipment. For new installations R-427A is not the choice; for R-22 retrofit work it remains commercially available.

    GWP-wise, R-427A is the lower-GWP option among the major R-22 retrofit family (R-407C at 1774 is lower, but requires POE oil change). The trade-offs across the R-22 retrofit family are: R-407C (lowest GWP, requires oil change), R-417A/R-421A/R-422D/R-427A/R-438A (moderate-to-higher GWP, mineral-oil compatible, no oil change).

  serviceNotes: |
    Compatible with mineral oil (MO), alkylbenzene (AB), and POE oils. For R-22 retrofits the existing mineral oil stays in the system — no oil change is required. R-427A has moderate temperature glide (~6-7°F at typical evaporator pressures); TXV operation may require attention after retrofit.

faqs:
  - q: "How does R-427A compare to other R-22 retrofit blends?"
    a: "R-427A has the lowest GWP (2138) among the major mineral-oil-compatible R-22 retrofit blends. R-417A (2346), R-421A (2630), R-422D (2729), R-438A (2265) are similar in function but higher GWP. R-407C (1774) is lower GWP than R-427A but requires POE oil change. R-427A's appeal is the combination of mineral-oil compatibility (no oil change) and the lowest GWP in that subset."

  - q: "What is the normal operating pressure of R-427A?"
    a: "Close to R-22 across the envelope. At 95°F outdoor ambient on a residential AC retrofitted from R-22 to R-427A, expect roughly 65-78 PSIG suction and 235-275 PSIG discharge — within a few PSI of R-22's operating envelope. The 15% R-32 content gives R-427A slightly higher capacity than the hydrocarbon-aided retrofits."

  - q: "What lubricant does R-427A use?"
    a: "Compatible with mineral oil (MO), alkylbenzene (AB), and POE. For R-22 retrofits the existing mineral oil stays in the system. R-427A doesn't include a hydrocarbon component — the mineral-oil compatibility comes from the blend chemistry itself."

  - q: "Does R-427A have temperature glide?"
    a: "Yes — about 6-7°F at typical evaporator pressures. Use the dew curve for superheat measurement. TXV systems may require retuning after retrofit; EXV systems handle the glide automatically."

  - q: "Is R-427A the same as Forane 427A?"
    a: "Yes. Forane 427A is Arkema's commercial brand name; R-427A is the ASHRAE 34 designation. Forane is Arkema's refrigerant brand family — Forane 22, Forane 410A, Forane 134a, etc., spanning the HFC and HCFC product lines."

  - q: "Should I use R-427A or R-407C for an R-22 retrofit?"
    a: "Trade-off between operational simplicity and GWP. R-407C requires POE oil change but has lower GWP (1774 vs 2138). R-427A works with the existing mineral oil but has higher GWP. For service-cost-sensitive retrofits R-427A is the simpler choice; for systems expected to remain in service long enough for the GWP difference to matter, R-407C is the cleaner choice."

  - q: "What's the GWP of R-427A?"
    a: "2138 (IPCC AR5, mass-weighted from R-32, R-125, R-143a, R-134a components). This is the lowest among the mineral-oil-compatible R-22 retrofit blends, though still above the AIM Act 700 threshold for new equipment."
