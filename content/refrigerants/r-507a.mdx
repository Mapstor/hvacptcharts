narrative:
  whatItIs: |
    R-507A is a binary azeotropic blend of 50% R-125 and 50% R-143a by mass. Unlike most refrigerant blends, R-507A behaves as a pure refrigerant — no temperature glide, single saturation pressure at each temperature. This made R-507A the preferred R-502 replacement for low-temperature commercial refrigeration applications where R-404A's small glide was operationally inconvenient.

    R-507A's GWP of 3985 is essentially identical to R-404A (3922) — both are very-high-GWP refrigerants and both face the same aggressive AIM Act phase-down. R-507A's only meaningful advantage over R-404A is the azeotropic behavior; in modern commercial refrigeration the replacement choices (R-448A, R-449A, R-454C, R-455A, R-744) all displace both.

  whereItsUsed:
    - Existing low-temperature commercial refrigeration (supermarket frozen food, walk-in freezers, ice machines)
    - Existing refrigerated transport
    - Service-only; new equipment in most categories has transitioned to alternatives

  phaseDownStatus: |
    R-507A is among the most aggressively phased-down refrigerants under the EPA AIM Act. The 2021 technology transition rule prohibits production and import of HFCs with GWP above 700 in new commercial refrigeration equipment effective January 1, 2025; R-507A at 3985 is well above this threshold.

    Reclaimed R-507A remains legal for servicing existing equipment indefinitely under current regulations. The 2029 and 2036 AIM Act reduction baselines (70% and 85% below the 2011-2013 baseline) will further constrain virgin supply.

  serviceNotes: |
    POE oil required. R-507A is azeotropic — no glide, single saturation pressure at each temperature. Service procedures are simpler than for zeotropic blends; gauge-based charging works correctly. Pressure ratings are similar to R-404A; service equipment is interchangeable from a pressure standpoint.

faqs:
  - q: "What does 'azeotropic' mean for R-507A?"
    a: "An azeotropic blend boils and condenses at a single temperature at constant pressure, behaving like a pure refrigerant in service. R-507A's specific R-125/R-143a 50/50 composition is the azeotropic point — at this exact ratio the two components evaporate together rather than fractionating. This makes R-507A operationally simpler than zeotropic blends like R-404A (which has ~2°F glide), R-407C (~7°F), or R-455A (~9°F)."

  - q: "How does R-507A compare to R-404A?"
    a: "R-507A is azeotropic (no glide); R-404A has small glide (~2°F). Both have similar capacity, similar operating pressures, similar GWP (R-507A 3985, R-404A 3922). Historically R-507A was preferred for applications where the small R-404A glide caused operational issues (precise charge measurement, very tight TXV control). Modern low-GWP A2L blends displace both equally."

  - q: "What is the normal operating pressure of R-507A?"
    a: "Very close to R-404A. At 0°F walk-in freezer evaporator with 95°F ambient, expect roughly 13-20 PSIG suction and 240-290 PSIG discharge. R-507A saturation at 70°F is 153 PSIG (single value — azeotropic). At -20°F (typical frozen food case): ~3 PSIG; at 25°F (walk-in cooler): ~36 PSIG."

  - q: "What lubricant does R-507A use?"
    a: "POE oil. Mineral oil and alkylbenzene are not miscible."

  - q: "What can replace R-507A in existing equipment?"
    a: "R-448A, R-449A for medium-temp applications (lower GWP, similar capacity). R-454C, R-455A for very low GWP. R-744 (CO2 transcritical) for centralized commercial refrigeration. R-290 (propane) for charge-limited self-contained equipment. As with R-404A, the retrofit procedure depends on whether the existing equipment is A2L-acceptable and whether oil changes are required."
