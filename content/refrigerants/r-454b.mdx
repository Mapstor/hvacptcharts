narrative:
  whatItIs: |
    R-454B is a near-azeotropic binary blend of 68.9% R-32 (difluoromethane) and 31.1% R-1234yf (HFO) by mass. The composition was engineered to closely match R-410A's saturation pressures across the residential and light commercial AC operating range while achieving a substantially lower GWP.

    R-32 contributes the high volumetric capacity and high pressure that match R-410A; R-1234yf contributes very low GWP and offsets R-32's discharge temperature. The result is a blend that operates within a few percent of R-410A's pressures, with negligible temperature glide (~0.4°F), and GWP of 466 — well below the EPA AIM Act 700 threshold.

  whereItsUsed:
    - Residential central air conditioning (new equipment from major US OEMs including Carrier)
    - Heat pumps (residential and light commercial)
    - Light commercial split systems
    - VRF/VRV systems (in the residential and light commercial segment)
    - Branded by Carrier as Puron Advance (continuation of the Puron R-410A brand)

  phaseDownStatus: |
    R-454B is not phase-down-targeted by the AIM Act — its GWP of 466 falls below the 700 threshold for new residential AC equipment. It is positioned as one of the long-term A2L replacements for R-410A in new equipment, alongside R-32 (GWP 675), R-452B (GWP 698), and R-454C (GWP 148).

    The EU F-Gas Regulation's 150 GWP threshold for stationary refrigeration would push toward lower-GWP options (R-454C at 148, R-455A at 148, R-516A at 142, or natural refrigerants) over the longer term — but in the US residential AC space, R-454B is the dominant A2L replacement choice for new Carrier equipment and a strong second to R-32 across the industry.

  serviceNotes: |
    Polyolester (POE) oil is required — the same as R-410A. Mineral oil and alkylbenzene are not miscible with R-454B and must not be used. POE is hygroscopic; pull vacuum to 500 microns and verify it holds for 30+ minutes before charging.

    A2L safety class requires: A2L-rated charge limits per UL 60335-2-40 and ASHRAE 15; refrigerant leak detection sensors; no open flames during service (brazing requires evacuation and venting per A2L procedures); A2L-rated recovery cylinders (yellow with red top stripe, like R-32 and R-1234yf, distinct from R-410A's pink); and technician training on A2L handling beyond standard Section 608 certification.

faqs:
  - q: "What is the normal operating pressure of R-454B?"
    a: "Very similar to R-410A — within about 5%. On a 95°F outdoor day with a properly-charged residential R-454B system, expect roughly 125–145 PSIG suction and 350–410 PSIG discharge. R-454B saturation at 70°F is ~190 PSIG bubble / ~183 PSIG dew. Service equipment rated for R-410A (800 PSI high-side) is pressure-appropriate for R-454B."

  - q: "How does R-454B compare to R-32?"
    a: "Both are A2L, both are POE-compatible, both are positioned as R-410A replacements. R-32 is a pure refrigerant with no glide; R-454B is a near-azeotrope (~0.4°F glide). R-32 GWP 675; R-454B GWP 466. R-32 has slightly higher volumetric capacity; R-454B operates at slightly lower discharge temperatures. The OEM split is roughly Daikin/Mitsubishi favoring R-32 (driven by their VRF and split product lines) and Carrier favoring R-454B (Puron Advance brand continuity)."

  - q: "Can I retrofit an R-410A system to R-454B?"
    a: "Not as a drop-in. While R-454B and R-410A use the same POE oil and operate at similar pressures, the A2L safety classification of R-454B requires equipment design changes: A2L-rated compressor and electrical components, integrated leak detection, charge limits per UL 60335-2-40. Retrofitting would require replacing the compressor and safety systems — at which point full equipment replacement with new R-454B-rated equipment is the standard path."

  - q: "What does the A2L safety classification mean for installers?"
    a: "Service procedures and equipment design must account for the mild flammability. No open flames in the operating environment (brazing requires the system to be evacuated). A2L-rated recovery cylinders and equipment (yellow with red top stripe). Charge limits depend on application — typically 1.84 kg / 4.06 lb maximum in unventilated rooms per UL 60335-2-40 for residential split systems; larger systems require A2L-compliant equipment design with sealed circuits. EPA Section 608 certification plus A2L-specific training are typical service prerequisites."

  - q: "What lubricant does R-454B use?"
    a: "Polyolester (POE) oil — the same as R-410A, R-32, and most modern HFC/HFO refrigerants. Mineral oil and alkylbenzene are not miscible and must not be used. POE is hygroscopic; vacuum integrity matters."

  - q: "Why is R-454B the right choice for new equipment instead of staying with R-410A?"
    a: "Regulation. The EPA AIM Act prohibited production of HFCs with GWP above 700 in new residential AC equipment effective January 1, 2025; R-410A at GWP 2088 does not comply. R-454B at GWP 466 does comply, operates at very similar pressures, uses the same POE oil, and is the OEM-favored choice for several major US residential AC brands."

  - q: "Where can I get R-454B refrigerant for service?"
    a: "Through standard refrigeration distributors; R-454B has been in commercial production since 2018 (initial Chemours Opteon XL41 launch) and is widely available in 25 lb and larger cylinders. Reclaimed R-454B will become more common as the installed base ages; for now most service material is virgin product. Cylinders are A2L-coded (yellow with red top stripe)."

    Direct retrofit is not recommended. The A2L safety classification requires equipment-level changes — compressor, electrical components, leak detection, charge limits — that effectively constitute a full equipment replacement. R-410A systems remain in service indefinitely under current regulations; reclaimed R-410A is the realistic service path for legacy equipment. For new installations the choice is among R-454B, R-32, R-452B, or (for very-low-GWP applications) R-454C — all A2L, all POE.
