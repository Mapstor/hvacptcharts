narrative:
  whatItIs: |
    R-1233zd(E) is pure (E)-1-chloro-3,3,3-trifluoro-1-propene — a hydrochlorofluoroolefin (HCFO) developed by Honeywell as the long-term replacement for R-123 in low-pressure centrifugal chillers. The molecule's structure (a chlorine atom and three fluorines on a propene backbone) reactive enough that atmospheric breakdown happens in weeks rather than decades, giving very low GWP (1) despite the chlorine content.

    R-1233zd(E) targets the same application envelope as R-123 (low-pressure centrifugal chillers for large commercial cooling) with the substantive improvements: A1 (lower toxicity than R-123's B1, easier machine-room requirements), GWP 1 (vs R-123's 77), and ODP 0.00034 (effectively zero vs R-123's 0.02). It's also broadly used as a foam-blowing agent for polyurethane insulation and as the working fluid for organic Rankine cycle (ORC) waste-heat recovery systems.

  whereItsUsed:
    - Low-pressure centrifugal chillers (large commercial cooling, district cooling) — the primary application
    - Foam blowing agent (polyurethane insulation)
    - Organic Rankine cycle working fluid (waste heat recovery)
    - Heat pump applications (some commercial high-temperature heat pumps)

  phaseDownStatus: |
    R-1233zd(E) is not phase-down-targeted. GWP 1 and ODP 0.00034 are below every regulatory threshold. The molecule is technically an HCFC (chlorine-containing) but its very short atmospheric lifetime (~26 days) means the chlorine doesn't accumulate in the stratosphere — ODP is effectively zero for practical purposes.

    For new low-pressure centrifugal chiller installations R-1233zd(E) is the dominant choice in 2026. Equipment OEMs (Carrier, Trane, York, Daikin) have standardized on R-1233zd(E) for their low-pressure chiller product lines as R-123 production ended in 2020.

  serviceNotes: |
    POE oil is required. The A1 safety class simplifies machine-room requirements compared to R-123's B1 — no special toxicity ventilation; standard ASHRAE 15 ventilation suffices for most installations. Operating pressures are very similar to R-123 (low-pressure, below atmospheric at typical evaporator temperatures); equipment service procedures parallel R-123 chiller service.

faqs:
  - q: "What is R-1233zd(E) also called?"
    a: "R-1233zd(E) is the ASHRAE 34 designation for the E-isomer (the more common form). Honeywell's commercial brands are Solstice 1233zd (refrigerant-grade) and Solstice LBA (Liquid Blowing Agent — foam blowing applications). The Z-isomer (R-1233zd(Z)) is a separate, less-common refrigerant for specialty applications."

  - q: "How does R-1233zd(E) compare to R-123?"
    a: "Same application envelope (low-pressure centrifugal chillers), substantively better environmental and safety profile. R-1233zd(E) A1 vs R-123 B1 (easier machine-room requirements). GWP 1 vs 77 (significantly lower climate impact). ODP 0.00034 vs 0.02 (effectively zero vs measurable). Operating pressures are very similar — both are low-pressure, both operate below atmospheric at typical chiller evaporator temperatures."

  - q: "Why is R-1233zd(E) classified as HCFC despite the very short atmospheric lifetime?"
    a: "Strict chemical classification. R-1233zd(E) contains chlorine, which puts it in the hydrochlorofluorocarbon family by composition. But the molecule's double bond makes it reactive enough that atmospheric breakdown happens within weeks — most chlorine is released at low altitudes where it doesn't reach the stratosphere. ODP measured by accepted methods is 0.00034 — effectively zero for regulatory and practical purposes. The chemical classification reflects composition; the environmental impact reflects fate."

  - q: "What lubricant does R-1233zd(E) use?"
    a: "Polyolester (POE) oil. Mineral oil and alkylbenzene are not miscible. POE is hygroscopic; deep vacuum matters before charging."

  - q: "Is R-1233zd(E) AIM Act affected?"
    a: "No. R-1233zd(E) is not a hydrofluorocarbon (HFC); it's a HCFO. The AIM Act targets HFC consumption specifically. R-1233zd(E)'s very low GWP also places it well below the AIM Act 700 threshold even if it were in scope."

  - q: "What is the normal operating pressure of R-1233zd(E)?"
    a: "Similar to R-123 — below atmospheric at typical chiller evaporator temperatures. Saturation at 70°F is approximately -3 PSIG (vacuum). At 100°F (typical condenser temperature): approximately 4 PSIG. The low-pressure operation is structural to the centrifugal chiller equipment design; not an indication of low charge."
