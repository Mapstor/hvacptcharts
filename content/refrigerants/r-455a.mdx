narrative:
  whatItIs: |
    R-455A is a ternary blend developed by Honeywell as the very-low-GWP A2L replacement for R-404A in commercial refrigeration. The composition includes a small CO2 component (3%) that contributes capacity and helps the blend stay below the EU F-Gas 150 threshold; R-32 (21.5%) and R-1234yf (75.5%) make up the bulk of the refrigerant.

    R-455A and R-454C (Chemours, 21.5/78.5 R-32/R-1234yf) occupy effectively the same market position — both A2L, both GWP ~148, both target R-404A retrofit. The CO2 component in R-455A contributes substantial temperature glide (~36 PSI / ~9°F at 70°F operating pressures), the largest among common low-GWP commercial refrigeration refrigerants.

  whereItsUsed:
    - Commercial refrigeration medium-temperature (supermarket cases, walk-in coolers)
    - Commercial refrigeration low-temperature (frozen-food cases, walk-in freezers)
    - R-404A retrofit (Honeywell's primary marketing position for L40X)
    - R-22 retrofit (commercial refrigeration applications)

  phaseDownStatus: |
    R-455A's GWP of 148 satisfies both the EPA AIM Act 700 threshold and the EU F-Gas Regulation 150 threshold for new stationary refrigeration. It's one of the very-low-GWP A2L options meeting the EU's long-term replacement objectives — alongside R-454C (148), R-516A (142), and natural refrigerants.

  serviceNotes: |
    Polyolester (POE) oil required. The CO2-containing blend has the largest temperature glide of common low-GWP commercial refrig blends (~9°F at typical evaporator pressures). Charge by weight per the equipment OEM specification; use the dew curve for superheat, bubble for subcooling. A2L safety procedures apply per UL 60335-2-89 and ASHRAE 15.

faqs:
  - q: "What is R-455A also called?"
    a: "R-455A is the ASHRAE 34 designation. Its commercial brand is Honeywell Solstice L40X — the 'L40X' name reflecting the blend's positioning as a low-GWP (L) successor to the R-404A (40) family."

  - q: "Why does R-455A have larger glide than R-454C?"
    a: "The CO2 component. R-454C is binary R-32/R-1234yf with components of similar volatility relative to each other. R-455A includes 3% CO2 which has a much lower boiling point (-78.5°C) than the R-32 (-51.7°C) or R-1234yf (-29.5°C) components. The wider volatility spread produces larger temperature glide (~9°F vs ~5°F for R-454C). For service work this means charging by weight rather than gauge, and using dew/bubble curves correctly."

  - q: "R-455A or R-454C for an R-404A retrofit?"
    a: "Both are A2L, both GWP 148, both meet EU F-Gas and EPA AIM Act thresholds. R-454C has smaller glide (~5°F vs R-455A's ~9°F), simplifying TXV operation. R-455A has the small CO2 component which contributes capacity for low-temperature applications. Manufacturer ecosystem (Honeywell Solstice L40X vs Chemours Opteon XL20) and OEM equipment compatibility lists drive the practical choice."

  - q: "What lubricant does R-455A use?"
    a: "Polyolester (POE) oil. Mineral oil and alkylbenzene are not miscible. POE is hygroscopic; deep vacuum matters before charging."

  - q: "What is the normal operating pressure of R-455A?"
    a: "At 0°F evaporator setpoint with 95°F ambient, expect roughly 15-22 PSIG suction and 240-285 PSIG discharge — comparable to R-404A's operating envelope. Saturation at 70°F is approximately 170 PSIG bubble / 121 PSIG dew — the large glide reflects the CO2 component's lower boiling point."
