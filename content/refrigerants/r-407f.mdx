narrative:
  whatItIs: |
    R-407F is Honeywell's commercial refrigeration HFC blend with composition tuned for lower GWP than R-404A while maintaining or exceeding R-404A's capacity. The 30% R-32 content (higher than R-407C's 23% or R-407A's 20%) drives both the capacity and the pressure envelope toward R-404A's operating window.

    R-407F sits between R-407A (GWP 2107) and R-407C (GWP 1774) in terms of GWP, while having the highest R-32 content of the R-407 family. The "Performax LT" branding emphasizes the low-temperature performance niche.

  whereItsUsed:
    - Commercial refrigeration medium and low temperature
    - R-404A retrofit (lower GWP, similar capacity)
    - R-22 retrofit (commercial refrigeration applications)

  phaseDownStatus: |
    R-407F's GWP of 1825 is above the EPA AIM Act 700 threshold for new equipment. For new commercial refrigeration installations the modern path is R-448A, R-449A, R-454C, R-455A, R-744, or R-290.

  serviceNotes: |
    POE oil required. Significant temperature glide (~5-6°F at typical operating pressures); TXV operation may require attention. Use the dew curve for superheat measurement.

faqs:
  - q: "How does R-407F compare to R-407C?"
    a: "Higher capacity, higher pressures, slightly higher GWP. R-407F is 30/30/40 (more R-32, more R-125, less R-134a) vs R-407C's 23/25/52. The higher R-32 content gives R-407F better volumetric capacity, making it more suitable for commercial refrigeration where R-407C falls short."

  - q: "Why use R-407F instead of R-404A?"
    a: "Lower GWP (1825 vs 3922) with similar capacity match for low-temperature commercial refrigeration. R-407F was Honeywell's intermediate-GWP option in the early 2010s before the very-low-GWP A2L blends (R-454C, R-455A) became broadly available. For new installations the modern A2L blends are preferred; for retrofit of existing R-404A systems where A1 non-flammable is critical, R-407F remains a meaningful GWP improvement."

  - q: "What lubricant does R-407F use?"
    a: "POE oil. Mineral oil and alkylbenzene are not miscible."

  - q: "What is the normal operating pressure of R-407F?"
    a: "Higher than R-407C across the envelope. At 25°F walk-in cooler evaporator with 95°F ambient, expect approximately 55-70 PSIG suction and 240-290 PSIG discharge. R-407F runs closer to R-404A pressures than R-407C does."

  - q: "Is R-407F still being sold?"
    a: "Yes, by Honeywell, with continuing US distributor availability for the R-404A retrofit market. AIM Act phase-down constraints will reduce supply over time as virgin HFC production caps reduce overall HFC consumption."

retrofitGuidance:
  fromRefrigerant: r-404a
  notes: |
    R-404A to R-407F retrofit: recover R-404A; verify POE oil; replace filter-drier; pull vacuum; charge R-407F by weight per system capacity (typically close to R-404A charge amount). Account for the glide in TXV adjustment — R-407F's ~5-6°F glide is meaningful vs R-404A's near-azeotropic behavior. Capacity should be comparable to R-404A; cooling output within 5% expected.
