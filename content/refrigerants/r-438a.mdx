narrative:
  whatItIs: |
    R-438A is Honeywell's R-22 retrofit blend — the Genetron MO99 product. The five-component composition includes the standard HFC trio (R-32, R-125, R-134a) plus two small hydrocarbon components (R-600 n-butane at 1.7%, R-601a isopentane at 0.6%) that aid miscibility between the HFC components and the mineral oil typical of R-22 systems.

    The hydrocarbon-aided mineral-oil compatibility is the key marketing position — the "MO99" name explicitly references mineral-oil compatibility and the ~99% capacity match to R-22 that Honeywell engineered into the blend. R-438A operates close to R-22's pressure envelope with similar capacity, simplifying the retrofit service call.

  whereItsUsed:
    - R-22 retrofit (residential AC, heat pumps, light commercial — the primary application)
    - Medium-temperature commercial refrigeration (R-22 retrofit)
    - Honeywell's preferred R-22 retrofit refrigerant; widely-stocked by HVAC distributors

  phaseDownStatus: |
    R-438A's GWP of 2265 is above the EPA AIM Act 700 threshold for new equipment. For new installations R-438A is not the choice; for R-22 retrofit work it remains commercially available and is one of the most widely-stocked retrofit blends in the US distribution channel.

    As the R-22 installed base ages out through equipment-replacement cycles, R-438A demand declines. New equipment installations use R-454B / R-32 / R-452B (residential AC) rather than refrigerant-only retrofits.

  serviceNotes: |
    Compatible with mineral oil (MO), alkylbenzene (AB), and POE oils — one of the most flexible R-22 retrofit blends in terms of lubricant compatibility. For R-22 retrofits the existing mineral oil typically stays in the system.

    R-438A has significant temperature glide (~10-12°F at typical operating pressures — the largest among the mineral-oil-compatible R-22 retrofit blends). TXV operation requires attention after retrofit; existing R-22 TXVs typically need adjustment or replacement for the larger glide. EXV is preferred.

faqs:
  - q: "What does the 'MO99' in the name mean?"
    a: "MO = mineral oil compatibility. 99 = approximately 99% capacity match to R-22 in residential AC applications. Honeywell's branding emphasizes both the operational simplicity (no oil change) and the close performance match to R-22 that the blend was engineered for."

  - q: "How does R-438A compare to other R-22 retrofit blends?"
    a: "R-438A is one of the higher-glide options (~10-12°F vs ~6-7°F for R-417A/R-422D/R-427A) due to the broader composition spread. This is the trade-off for the close capacity match. GWP-wise R-438A (2265) sits in the middle of the R-22 retrofit family — lower than R-422A (3143) and R-422D (2729), higher than R-427A (2138) and R-417A (2346). All are mineral-oil-compatible and follow the same general retrofit procedure."

  - q: "What is the normal operating pressure of R-438A?"
    a: "Close to R-22 in residential AC applications — Honeywell engineered the blend for ~99% capacity match. At 95°F outdoor ambient on a residential AC retrofitted from R-22 to R-438A, expect roughly 65-78 PSIG suction and 235-275 PSIG discharge — within a few PSI of R-22's operating envelope. The capacity match means cooling output is comparable to original R-22 performance."

  - q: "What lubricant does R-438A use?"
    a: "Compatible with mineral oil (MO), alkylbenzene (AB), and POE. For R-22 retrofits the existing mineral oil stays in the system — this is the structural appeal of R-438A and the other 'MO99-style' retrofit blends. The R-600 and R-601a hydrocarbon components in R-438A aid HFC-mineral-oil miscibility."

  - q: "Why does R-438A have larger glide than other R-22 retrofits?"
    a: "The five-component composition spans a wider range of volatilities. The R-32 component (NBP -51.7°C), R-125 (-48.5°C), R-134a (-26°C), R-600 (-0.5°C), and R-601a (+27.8°C) cover a much larger boiling-point range than three-component blends. The chemistry of zeotropic blends produces temperature glide proportional to the volatility spread of the components."

  - q: "Should I use R-438A or R-407C for an R-22 retrofit?"
    a: "Trade-off similar to R-427A vs R-407C. R-438A: mineral-oil compatible (no oil change), GWP 2265, larger glide. R-407C: requires POE oil change, GWP 1774, somewhat smaller glide. For service-cost-sensitive retrofits R-438A is simpler; for lower long-term climate impact R-407C is cleaner. Both follow the same general retrofit procedure once the oil-change decision is made."

  - q: "Is R-438A still being manufactured?"
    a: "Yes, by Honeywell, with widespread US distributor availability. AIM Act phase-down constraints reduce supply over time as virgin HFC production caps reduce overall HFC consumption. For R-22 retrofit work R-438A remains the Honeywell-stocked default; reclaimed supply will support continued service for retrofits performed in the next several years."

retrofitGuidance:
  fromRefrigerant: r-22
  notes: |
    R-22 to R-438A retrofit per Honeywell Genetron MO99 service literature: recover R-22; replace filter-drier with HFC-compatible model; pull vacuum to 500 microns held 30 minutes; charge R-438A by weight per the system's calculated capacity (typically 90-100% of original R-22 charge weight). Mineral oil stays in the system. The large glide (~10-12°F) typically requires TXV adjustment after retrofit; some installations move from TXV to EXV for better glide handling. Capacity match to R-22 should produce cooling output within 5% of original.
