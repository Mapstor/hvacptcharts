narrative:
  whatItIs: |
    R-422D is a ternary blend in the ISCEON family of R-22 retrofit refrigerants — a "no oil change" option specifically tuned for medium-temperature commercial AC and heat pump applications. The composition (65.1% R-125 / 31.5% R-134a / 3.4% R-600a) provides closer capacity match to R-22 at residential AC operating conditions than R-422A (which is tuned for low-temperature refrigeration).

    The 3.4% R-600a hydrocarbon component is the structural enabler of mineral-oil compatibility — it aids miscibility between the HFC refrigerant and the mineral oil typical of R-22 systems, allowing R-422D retrofit without the POE oil change that R-407C requires.

  whereItsUsed:
    - R-22 retrofit (residential AC, heat pumps, light commercial — the primary application)
    - Medium-temperature commercial refrigeration (R-22 retrofit)
    - Legacy R-22 equipment service where oil change is impractical

  phaseDownStatus: |
    R-422D's GWP of 2729 is above the EPA AIM Act 700 threshold for new equipment. For new installations R-422D is not the choice; for R-22 retrofit work it remains commercially available.

    Compared to R-407C (GWP 1774, requires POE oil change): R-422D trades higher GWP for operational simplicity (no oil change). The choice depends on whether oil-change avoidance outweighs the GWP improvement in the specific service context.

  serviceNotes: |
    Compatible with mineral oil (MO), alkylbenzene (AB), and POE oils. For R-22 retrofits the existing mineral oil typically stays in the system. R-422D has moderate temperature glide (~6-7°F at typical AC operating pressures); TXV systems may require retuning after retrofit.

faqs:
  - q: "How does R-422D compare to R-422A?"
    a: "Same blend family (ISCEON) with different compositions tuned for different temperature applications. R-422A (85.1/11.5/3.4) is tuned for low-temperature commercial refrigeration. R-422D (65.1/31.5/3.4) is tuned for medium-temperature residential and commercial AC. Both use the hydrocarbon component for mineral-oil compatibility. The lower R-125 content of R-422D gives it lower GWP (2729 vs 3143) and better capacity match at AC operating conditions."

  - q: "What is the normal operating pressure of R-422D?"
    a: "Close to R-22 across the residential AC envelope. At 95°F outdoor ambient on a residential AC retrofitted from R-22 to R-422D, expect roughly 65-78 PSIG suction and 230-275 PSIG discharge — within a few PSI of R-22's operating envelope. The capacity match is good for AC applications (the primary use case)."

  - q: "What lubricant does R-422D use?"
    a: "Compatible with mineral oil (MO), alkylbenzene (AB), and POE. For R-22 retrofits the existing mineral oil stays in the system. The 3.4% R-600a hydrocarbon component aids HFC-mineral-oil miscibility — this is the structural difference vs R-407C and R-422A blends without hydrocarbon components."

  - q: "Does R-422D have temperature glide?"
    a: "Yes — about 6-7°F at typical AC operating pressures. This is comparable to R-407C and meaningful for TXV operation. EXV systems handle the glide automatically; TXV systems may require expansion-valve adjustment after retrofit. Fixed-orifice systems are less affected. Use the dew curve for superheat measurement (the site's superheat calculator handles this automatically)."

  - q: "Should I use R-422D or R-417A for an R-22 retrofit?"
    a: "Both are mineral-oil-compatible R-22 retrofits with hydrocarbon components. R-417A (Chemours, 46.6/50.0/3.4 R-125/R-134a/R-600): GWP 2346, slightly closer capacity match to R-22. R-422D (Chemours, 65.1/31.5/3.4 R-125/R-134a/R-600a): GWP 2729, slightly lower pressures than R-22. Both follow the same retrofit procedure; the choice is largely about distributor availability and contractor familiarity rather than performance."

  - q: "What's the GWP of R-422D?"
    a: "2729 (IPCC AR5, mass-weighted). For context: R-22 GWP is 1810, R-407C GWP is 1774. R-422D's higher GWP is the trade-off for mineral-oil compatibility and its specific capacity-tuning for AC applications. The retrofit value is operational continuity rather than climate-impact reduction."

  - q: "Is R-422D the same as MO29?"
    a: "Yes. MO29 (or ISCEON MO29) is the commercial brand name; R-422D is the ASHRAE 34 designation. The 'MO' in the name reflects the mineral-oil-compatibility marketing position. Chemours acquired the ISCEON brand from DuPont; current cylinders are typically labeled Chemours ISCEON MO29."
